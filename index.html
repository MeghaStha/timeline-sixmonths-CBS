National Housing Reconstruction Program

First meeting convened for the project

The Government of Nepal declares 14 districts as the most earthquake affected districts and decides to conduct a detailed door to-door survey using mobile data collection technology. This survey would collect: Data on physical damage to residential buildings, and Demographic and socio-economic data of its residents.

October 2015

HRD Collect App demo at Central Bureau of Statistics

KLL develops and demonstrates the initial version of HRD Collect - the mobile/tablet application developed to conduct this survey.


November 1, 2015

First Pilot: Survey in Khokana VDC of Lalitpur District

HRD Collect is field tested in Khokana VDC of Lalitpur District. During the intervening time, the app goes through multiple rounds of iterations in which several new questions and features are added to it.

December 5, 2015

Pilot Enumerator's Training

26 enumerators are trained for the full-scale pilot survey.

December 15, 2015

Second Pilot: Survey in Ravi Opi VDC of Kavrepalanchowk District

In this two-day pilot, 79 records are collected and successfully uploaded to the central server from the field.

December 16 - 17, 2015

Master Trainer for Trainees (MToT) Training Starts

40 people are trained as MToT (Master Trainer of Trainers). KLL trains them on tablet’s basic operations, HRD Collect and tablet dos and don’ts.

December 21, 2015

Training to 127 Enumerators: First batch of Enumerators to go to field (Dolakha)

127 enumerators are trained and deployed to Dolakha district for the first full-scale survey.

December 27, 2015

KLL helpdesk starts

KLL HelpDesk is set up and starts providing remote telephonic assistance to on-field enumerators on matters pertaining to tablets and HRD Collect app. By mid-April, this HelpDesk handled 995 registered (logged) calls and about another 100 unlogged calls.

January 1, 2016

Server at GIDC: Received first survey upload from Dolakha

Completed setup of makeshift server at GIDC (Government Integrated Data Center) at Singha Durbar; This sever would hold the survey data uploaded from field. We received first survey upload from Dolakha field on this day.

January 10, 2016

Enumerators Training: 400 Enumerators trained all at the same time (First of the three)

First of the three trainings where 400+ enumerators are trained at a time - with 10 training sessions running concurrently.

January 18, 2016

Release of nhrpprogress.gov.np

This interactive web portal uses maps and charts as its primary interfaces to communicate latest survey statistics based on the data received on the central data server.

Feburary 12, 2016

All 1500+ enumerators on field

For the first time, all 1500+ enumerators are on the field at a time.

Feburary 12, 2016

Enumerators from the first batch start to return from field (Dolakha)

The first clearance is also conferred to enumerator returning from Dolakha at CBS, Kathmandu.

Feburary 19, 2016

Congestion at the GIDC server

Increasing congestion is observed at the central server due to high numbers of concurrent upload attempts from field. Situation worsens as the week progresses with the number of pending uploads keeps mounting.

March 14, 2016

Tech team meeting at KLL

KLL convenes a Tech Workshop at KLL office to resolve slow upload issues; An additional server is setup and server-side optimizations are deployed in order to solve the issue.

March 23, 2016

33000 records received by GIDC server in one day

The two servers combined receive 33,000+ records on this day. It is the highest numbers of records uploaded on any single day. The two servers continue to receive in excess of 20,000 records per day for the next few days while the numbers of pending uploads keep shrinking.

March 24, 2016

Field work continues
